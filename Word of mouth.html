" Hitting the right people at exactly the right time"

Forget demographics

Most marketers define their brand’s core media target by demographics. But this is just a hangover from the TV era. Digital media use behavior to hit people much more efficiently. If you are selling LCD televisions, your core target is not men, or people aged 25 to 35 or ABC1s. It is people who are actively researching a planned LCD television purchase. In the digital era, marketing is getting back to basics. Your core target is the people who are thinking of buying from you.

Offer solutions when they have problems

Washing machine manufacturers advertise in vain on analog television because no one cares about washing machines. The only time when people do care about washing machines is when theirs is broken. Getting their attention is easy when their machine is flooding their apartment. That’s when you should hit them.

Forget the long term

Why talk about decade-long brand building any more anyway? Yahoo became a global brand within two years of its launch; MySpace and YouTube took less. In today’s connected world, brand building is something that happens rapidly, or not at all.
